Peoplesized solutions

Building things that work for humans, not institutions

Make work easier for everyone

Too many tools are designed to make managers' jobs easier while making everyone else's harder. Real solutions reduce friction for the people doing the actual work—not just those watching from above.

Fit people's real workflows

Instead of forcing people to adapt to rigid systems, design solutions that adapt to how people actually work. Meet them where they are, with tools that feel natural and intuitive.

Value human judgment over process

Processes should support good decisions, not replace them. The best solutions amplify human insight rather than constraining it with unnecessary bureaucracy.

Start simple, stay simple

Complexity compounds. Begin with the smallest thing that could work, and resist the urge to pile on features. Simple solutions scale; complicated ones break.

I spent years building solutions for environmental and social challenges. Along the way, I learned that the best technology gets out of people's way and helps them do what they do best.
